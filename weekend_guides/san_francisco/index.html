Welcome to San Francisco, a 7 mile by 7 mile city nestled on a fist shaped peninsula often embraced by a deep, beautiful fog. With its hilly terrain and multitude of neighborhoods, there is always something to eat and somewhere to go.

Must do things

San Francisco has a lot to offer - between the food and the sights, the options are unlimited. Here are a few things that are a must do:

  • A. Lands End: This park on the edge of the city offers amazing views of the Golden Gate Bridge. The rocky shoreline and beaches are amazing spots to snap some photos. Plan to spend a couple of hours hiking and hopefully spotting some whales.
    Price: free
    Hours: 9am-5pm everyday
    Contact: National Park Service, + 1 (415) 426-5240
    Location: 680 Point Lobos Ave, San Francisco, CA
  • B. Union Square/Market Street: Exploring Union Square is a great way to get acquainted with the city's hilly nature and the perfect gateway to exploring Chinatown and Market Street. If you have time, checkout the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art.
    Price: free
    Hours: 24 hours
    Contact: Union Square SF, + 1 (415) 781-7880
    Location: 333 Post St, San Francisco, CA
  • C. The Mission & Dolores Park: The Mission District is the city's most exuberant neighborhood with some of its newest and hippest restaurants and bars. On a sunny weekend day head to Dolores Park with a beach towel and six pack to enjoy the crowds and sun. Make sure to grab a bite at Tartine Bakery and some ice-cream at Bi-rite Creamery!
    Price: free
    Hours: 6am-10pm everyday
    Contact: SF Rec & Park, + 1 (415) 554-9521
    Location: Dolores St & 19th St, San Francisco, CA
  • D. Twin Peaks: Twin Peaks provides an exhilarating vantage point of the city and bay area. Whether you hike up there or take and Uber like me, it's a great time. Just remember to bring warm clothing.
    Price: free
    Hours: 5am-midnight everyday
    Contact: SF Rec & Park, + 1 (415) 831-2700
    Location: 501 Twin Peaks Blvd, San Francisco, CA

Where to Eat

San Fransisco is filled with restaurants from all cuisines and cultures. Here are some places that are worth your time:

  • E. Brenda's French Soul Food ($$) : The line is often long for a reason. The food is amazing, full of flavours that will leave your mouth watering. The beignets are wonderful balls of joy and the fried chicken is heaven.
    Hours: 8am-3pm Monday & Tuesday, 8am-10pm Wednesday to Saturday, 8am-8pm Sunday
    Contact: Website, + 1 (415) 345-8100
    Location: 652 Polk St, San Francisco, CA
  • F. Ramen Yamadaya ($$) : Located in Japantown, Ramen Yamadaya serves the best ramen in the city. The creamy broth is simmered for 20-hours and leaves if full of authentic flavours. The spiciness can be adjusted to your preference.
    Hours: 11:30am–3pm, 5pm–10pm Monday to Friday, 11:30am-10pm Saturday & Sunday
    Contact: Website, + 1 (415) 795-4164
    Location: 1728 Buchanan St, San Francisco, CA
  • G. Nopalito ($$) : This restaurant serves authentic Mexican food at a reasonable price with great service. The food is definitely Instagrammable and tastes even better than it looks.
    Hours: 11:30am–10pm everyday
    Contact: Website, + 1 (415) 437-0303
    Location: 306 Broderick St, San Francisco, CA
  • H. Yank Sing ($$$) : Although the prices are steep, the food is worth it. Everything from the rice to the dumpling and vegetables are scrumptious.
    Hours: 11am–3pm Monday to Friday, 10am-4pm Saturday & Sunday
    Contact: Website, + 1 (415) 541-4949
    Location: 49 Stevenson St, San Francisco, CA
  • I. Taquería El Farolito ($) : At this small and crowded restaurant, you can find the best burritos in the Bay Area.
    Hours: 10am–3am Monday to Saturday, 10am-midnight Sunday
    Contact: Website, + 1 (415) 541-4949
    Location: 2779 Mission St, San Francisco, CA
